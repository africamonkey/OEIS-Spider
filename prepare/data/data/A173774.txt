        "On Feb. 10, 2010 Zhi-Wei Sun introduced the sequence and conjectured that each term a(n) is an integer divisible by 4*binom(2n,n). On Feb. 11, 2011 Kasper Andersen confirmed this conjecture by noting that the sequence b(n)=a(n)/(4*binom(2n,n)) (n=1,2,3,...) coincides with the sequence A112029 at OEIS. Zhi-Wei Sun proved that for every prime p and positive integer a we have a(p^a)=8+16p^3B_{p-3} (mod p^4), where B_0,B_1,B_2,... are Bernoulli numbers. Given a prime p, Zhi-Wei Sun conjectured that sum_{k=0}^{(p-1)/2}(21k+8)binom(2k,k)^3=8p+(-1)^{(p-1)/2}32p^3E_{p-3} (mod p^4) if p>3 (where E_0,E_1,E_2,... are Euler numbers), and that sum_{k=0}^{[2p^a/3]}(21k+8)binom(2k,k)^3=8p^a (mod p^{a+5+(-1)^p}) if a is a positive integer with p^a=1 (mod 3). He also observed that b(n)=a(n)/(4*binom(2n,n)) is odd if and only if n is a power of two."
