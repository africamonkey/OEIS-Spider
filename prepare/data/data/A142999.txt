        "a(n) = n!^2*sum {k = 1..n} (-1)^(k+1)/k^2. Recurrence: a(0) = 0, a(1) = 1, a(n+1) = (2*n+1)*a(n) + n^4*a(n-1). The sequence b(n):= n!^2 satisfies the same recurrence with the initial conditions b(0) = 1, b(1) = 1. Hence we obtain the finite continued fraction expansion a(n)/b(n) = 1/(1+ 1^4/(3+ 2^4/(5+ 3^4/(7+...+ (n-1)^4/(2*n-1))))), for n >=2.
