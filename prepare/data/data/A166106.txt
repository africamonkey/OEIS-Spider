Here, let us refer it as a recursive Fibonacci tree. A Fibonacci number F(n) is the weight of the root of a weighted recursive Fibonacci tree of order n in which the leafs have a weight of 1, and the weight of a node equals the sum of the weights of its two children. If instead we weight each leaf by the number of nodes above it (considering the root as the top-most node), then for n>2, a(n) is the weight of the root of such a weighted tree of order n.
